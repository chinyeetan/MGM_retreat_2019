Introduction

  • The search for commensal species in humans which promote health and abrogate disease has been a high-priority but onerous venture.
  • Identification of a single taxon or small consortia of taxa that promote human health will spur development of probiotic drugs for a range of diseases.
  • Host-microbe triangulation pioneered by Surana and Kasper (2017) identified a hitherto unknown taxon of bacteria strongly associated with a protective phenotype in mouse colitis; subsequent culture and characterization led to the discovery of a new species, designated Clostridium immunis.

Figure 1: The first image of C.immunis captured by Transmission Electron Microscopy.

Approach

  • Immunomodulatory effects of C.immunis were assessed using classical disease models with robust phenotypic readout.
  • Finding out the ‘where’ and ‘when’ of C.immunis colonization will educate the search for mechanistic clues.
  • Genetic manipulation of C.immunis is critical to identify genetically-encoded functions of its effects.

Results

Oral administration of C. immunis mitigates clinical severity in two distinct mouse models of inflammatory disease

Figure 2: C. immunis gavage ameliorates lethality in DSS colitis (L) and clinical severity in experimental autoimmune encephalitis (R).

C. immunis is localized to the cecum and proximal-middle colon
